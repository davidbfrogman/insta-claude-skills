## Full script (~100 sec)

Stanford just dropped their 2026 AI report — 425 pages. I read every one so you don't have to, and honestly, a few of these you really don't want to scroll past.

I've spent 20 years in tech, and this first one still caught me off guard. Right now everybody's racing to "get good at ChatGPT." Prompt courses, prompt cheat sheets, the whole thing. And that's not wrong exactly — it's just already behind. Because when Stanford looked at what companies are actually hiring for, the demand is moving somewhere else entirely.

Here's the number that stopped me: postings mentioning chatbots and conversational AI are declining. But postings for AI agents and orchestration — tools that chain models together to actually get work done — grew over ten thousand percent in a single year. Ten thousand. That's not a trend, that's a stampede.

So what does that mean for you? Writing a good prompt is table stakes now — everybody's got that. The thing that actually gets you hired is knowing how to string multiple AI agents into a workflow that runs on its own. One agent researches, one drafts, one checks the work — and you're the person who designed the whole machine. That's the skill nobody's teaching in those prompt courses.

And you don't need a CS degree to start. You need one real workflow you can point to. I put together a short guide on exactly how to build your first one — comment "AGENTS" and I'll send it straight to you.

This is just one of ten. I'm breaking down every insight from this report over the series — what it really says about jobs, careers, and where to point yourself for 2026. Follow so you don't miss the rest.

## Guide / lead-magnet strategy

**The problem:** if the guide contains all 10 insights, there's no reason to follow for the rest of the series.

**The fix — per-insight micro-guide, delivered by comment keyword:**
- The **reel** delivers the *what* (the insight). The **guide** delivers the *how* — an actionable companion to *that one insight only*. It expands; it never spoils the other nine.
- Insight #1 guide = "How to build your first AI agent workflow" (step-by-step). Keyword: **AGENTS**.
- Mechanic: comment-a-keyword → auto-DM (ManyChat-style). This spikes comments/reach, captures the lead, and keeps insights 2–10 behind the follow.

**Why not the alternatives:**
- *All-10 guide:* hands over the whole series, kills the follow incentive.
- *"Check the caption":* low friction but no engagement signal and no lead capture — fallback only.

**Lighter-load option:** instead of 10 separate polished guides, release one evolving "2026 AI Career Playbook," one new section per reel to people who commented. Same follow incentive, less upfront build.
