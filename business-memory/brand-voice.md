# Brand Voice

### Voice in three adjectives
*Pick three words that describe how your brand sounds. Then add a short "so we…" for each so the
words aren't just vague.*

> *Example (replace me):*
> - **Warm** — so we write like a friendly regular greeting you, not a corporation.
> - **Knowledgeable** — so we share real coffee expertise without lecturing.
> - **Unpretentious** — so we never make anyone feel dumb for not knowing coffee jargon.

### Tone (and how it shifts by context)
*Your baseline tone, plus how it flexes for different situations. Tone changes; voice stays constant.*

> *Example (replace me):* Baseline: friendly and relaxed. On social media, more playful and a little
> witty. In a customer complaint, sincere and accountable — we drop the jokes. In product/menu copy,
> descriptive and appetizing, never salesy.

### Words & phrases we use
*Signature words, phrases, and grammatical choices — including pronouns. Small habits define a voice.*

> *Example (replace me):*
> - We say **"we"** and **"our"** — "our roaster," "we pulled this shot for you." Never "the company."
> - We call customers **"neighbors,"** not "guests" or "consumers."
> - Words we love: *small-batch, fresh, ritual, craft, cozy, neighborhood.*
> - We greet with "Morning!" and sign off with "See you soon."

### Words & phrases we avoid
*Words that feel off-brand — corporate speak, overhyped claims, or anything that clashes with your voice.*

> *Example (replace me):* Avoid: *synergy, world-class, disrupt, best-in-class, cheap, discount,*
> exclamation-point overload, and coffee snob jargon we don't explain (e.g. "washed Gesha" without context).

### Grammar & style rules
*Your conventions so writing stays consistent: capitalization, punctuation, emoji, contractions, etc.*

> *Example (replace me):*
> - Use contractions (we're, it's) — we write how we talk.
> - Sentence case for headings, not Title Case.
> - Emoji: sparingly on social (☕ 🌱 okay), never in email subject lines.
> - Oxford comma: yes. Exclamation points: at most one per message.

### Example rewrites (off-brand → on-brand)
*Show one or two before/after pairs. Examples teach voice faster than rules do.*

> *Example (replace me):*
> - ❌ Off-brand: "Purchase our premium artisanal coffee products today for an unparalleled experience!"
> - ✅ On-brand: "Fresh beans, roasted this week. Come grab a bag — we'll tell you what we're loving right now."

## Visual Identity

### Colors
*Your brand colors with hex codes if you have them. Note which is primary vs. accent, and the vibe.*

> *Example (replace me):*
> - Primary: deep espresso brown `#3B2A20`
> - Secondary: warm cream `#F4EBDD`
> - Accent: ember orange `#D2622A`
> - Overall vibe: earthy, warm, natural.

### Typography
*Your fonts, or the style of font you use, for headlines and body text.*

> *Example (replace me):* Headlines in a friendly rounded serif (like Cooper or Recoleta); body in a
> clean humanist sans-serif. Nothing sharp or corporate.

### Logo usage
*How your logo should and shouldn't be used, if it matters to you.*

> *Example (replace me):* Wordmark "Ember & Oak" with a small flame-over-leaf icon. Keep clear space
> around it; don't stretch, recolor, or put it on busy photos.

### Imagery style
*The look and feel of your photos and visuals.*

> *Example (replace me):* Natural light, warm tones, real people and real hands, a little imperfect and
> candid. Show steam, texture, the shop's wood and plants. Avoid sterile stock photography.
