# Business Memory

A set of five short documents that give Claude Co-Work standing context about your business —
who your customers are, how your brand speaks, how your product is positioned, and how you sell.

Fill these out **once**. Then, at the start of any Co-Work session, drop this whole folder in (or
point Claude at it) and Claude will understand your business without you re-explaining it every time.

## How to use

1. Fill out each file below. Every section has a short *instruction* telling you what to write and
   why, followed by an example you can learn from and then **delete**.
2. The examples all describe the same fictional business — **Ember & Oak Coffee**, a small specialty
   coffee shop — so you can see how the pieces fit together. Replace every example with your own.
3. Keep them current. Revisit once a quarter, or whenever your offering, pricing, or audience shifts.

## The five pillars

Fill them out in this order — each builds on the one before it:

1. **[company-overview.md](company-overview.md)** — the anchor. What you do, why you exist, what
   makes you different. Start here.
2. **[ideal-customer-profile.md](ideal-customer-profile.md)** — who you serve, their pain points,
   and the exact words they use to describe them.
3. **[brand-voice.md](brand-voice.md)** — how you sound and look: tone, vocabulary, and visual identity.
4. **[product-and-positioning.md](product-and-positioning.md)** — what you sell, how you talk about
   it, and how you stand apart. Includes your marketing channels.
5. **[sales-strategy.md](sales-strategy.md)** — how you acquire and close customers, and what you
   need to know about a lead.

## A note on the examples

Anywhere you see a line like this, it is an example for you to replace:

> *Example (replace me):* We roast small-batch coffee for people who care where their beans come from.

Delete the example and the `*Example (replace me):*` marker once you've written your own answer. If a
section doesn't apply to you, write "N/A" rather than leaving the example in — an example left behind
will confuse Claude into thinking it's true about your business.
