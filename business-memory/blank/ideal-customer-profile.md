# Ideal Customer Profile

### Primary customer segment(s)
*Name your 1–3 most important types of customer. Give each a short label and a one-line description.
If you serve businesses, describe the company AND the person who buys.*

→

### Demographics / firmographics
*Concrete attributes of your core customer: age, income, location, role, company size, industry —
whatever is relevant to your business.*

→

### Jobs-to-be-done / goals
*What is the customer really trying to accomplish? Look past the product to the underlying goal.*

→

### Pain points
*The frustrations, problems, or unmet needs that drive them to you. What are they trying to get away from?*

→

### The exact language customers use *(most important section)*
*Verbatim phrases customers actually say or write — in reviews, in conversation, in DMs. Do not
polish or paraphrase. This is the raw language Claude should echo back in marketing and replies.*

→

### Buying triggers
*The moments or events that make someone become a customer, or buy more. When do they show up?*

→

### Where they hang out
*The channels, platforms, and places where you can actually reach these people.*

→

### Common objections / hesitations
*Why a good-fit customer might NOT buy, or might hesitate. Be honest about your friction.*

→

### Who is NOT a fit (anti-persona)
*Describe the customer you should NOT chase — it keeps your messaging sharp and saves you from
bad-fit business.*
